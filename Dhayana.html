Dhayana

Dhayana (Meditation) :-

This is the seventh part of Ashtanga yoga. Mind must be doing this work during the whole time of meditation without deviation. Beginners will be having the experience of deviation of mind several times. Then push out other thoughts if any in the mind and again bring the given work again. Meditation can be done silently or with sound. Silent meditation is not disturbing any one. Therefore it can be done anywhere. Sound meditation will help us to avoid outside sound and we get more concentration. After attaining concentration, chakra meditation can be practiced by bringing mind on each chakra. This will enable us to control the function of each chakra to stop and start according to our will. Soonya meditation is deeper than this which is making the conscious level of mind sleep. Then the mind will be like surface of water without ripples or flame of candle where there is no wind. In this level as the conscious level is not working, we can enter into the subconscious level of mind to understand who we are, what we were and what we will become. This is self realization. But this level is not easy to reach. The immediate result we can enjoy is another. One who is practicing meditation for even two or three years can have a mind always with positive thoughts, which will bring only happiness. Negative thoughts like anxiety, fear, prejudice, hatred etc. will bring only worries. For any new thing the mind has four steps registration, categorization, stimulation and reaction. One who is practicing meditation will not give reaction for a negative stimulus. Then the mind gets a gap between stimulus and reaction and mind moves slowly to positive side. The reaction for a negative stimulus will be unhealthy, while positive stimulus will tempt for healthy reaction.
