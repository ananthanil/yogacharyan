Diet of a Yogi

Positive and Negative Foods

The food that we take has great influence on the thought. If we take positive food, our thought also will be always positive and keep our body and mind healthy. The nature has provided food for all living beings around them. In the forest, there are many animals. Each animal will take only the food allotted by the nature. A lion will not take the grass of a deer or fruits of a monkey. The monkey or deer will never take the meat of an animal which is the food of a lion. They all are leading a very healthy life and need not have the help of a doctor for treatment of any disease. The domestic animals get diseased by taking the artificial food we give. How can we distinguish between positive and negative food?

Getting the sight or smell of some food like jack fruit, pineapple, mango, water will be coming to the mouth. We will not get this experience by seeing a fish in the aquarium or a goat on the wayside. The water in the mouth is the first digestive juice. The body is getting ready for digesting the food if you take that positive food. But the body is not reacting by the sight of fish or goat. It is not our food. A lion by the sight of a goat will have the saliva in his mouth. It is his food. By the sight of pineapple he will not get salivation. Thus the body is distinguishing the positive and negative food.

A yogi, who is practicing yoga all his life, needs only one meal a day and it can be lunch. All others can have two meals, breakfast and lunch. By taking three meals we actually get diseased. Night eating is not good. We are day beings. During night digestion is low. The meals we take during night will not be fully digested and will cause diseases. Even the day birds stop their eating by sunset and go to their nests. When the stomach is full the body will give a sign to stop eating with a belching. If you continue to eat, another belching will warn you not to eat. A third belching will bring some food also with it to the mouth.
