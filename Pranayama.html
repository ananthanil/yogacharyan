Pranayama :-(Controlled breathing)

This is the fourth part of Ashtanga Yoga. When we breath we assimilate oxygen and eliminate carbon dioxide. This is the physiological aspect of breathing. In yoga there is one more aspect. We are receiving cosmic energy and convert it into bio -magnetic energy. This is our life energy which we store in seven energy points on our body called Chakras. Five of these points are on spinal cord which is a continuation of brain coming down to the end of back bone. This is the first point namely Mooladhara (base chakra). Its prime function is excretion and is in the shape of lotus with four red petals. The second behind the sex organ is Swadhishtana (sex chakra). It has six petals of orange colour and its function is reproduction. Till the age of puberty it will be in dormant stage. Manipooraka the third is behind the navel (navel chakra) has ten yellow petals and its prime function is assimilation. Anahatha (heart chakra) has twelve green petals and it is the point of higher emotions. Unkind people are called heartless because this chakra is not properly activated in them. Hearty congratulation and hearty welcome are emerging from this point. The fifth point Visudhi (throat chakra) is the point of speech and language having sixteen blue petals. Ajna the (eye brow chakra) at the middle of eye brows having two indigo petals is the point of reasoning power. Sahasrara the seventh (crown chakra) with thousand violet petals is at the top of the head. This point can be activated only with yoga and meditation and helps us in self realization which is the ultimate aim of yoga.

Pranayama has four steps. Pooraka (breath in), anthara kumbhaka (hold in), Rechaka (breath out) and Bahyakumbhaka (hold out). Beginners are given only two steps breath in and breath out in 1:2 by adjusting breathing out time nearly double the breathing in can be 3+6 or 4+8. The second step can be taken after few weeks.

Here holding in also introduced and the exercise is given as 4+4+4, four seconds to breath in four seconds to hold in and four seconds to breath out. After some weeks the holding time and breathing out time can be increased, as 4+5+5...4+6+6...4+7+7and 4+8+8. The holding time can be increased further as 4+9+8.4+10+8.... up to 4+16+8.

When introducing holding out time it starts by 4+4+4+4 and can be enhanced up to 4+8+8+4. Five vayus and five upavayus are working in the body. Prana, udana, apana, samana and vyana are the main vayus. The first four are working in heart, throat base and navel chakras respectively. Vyana the fifth spreads all over the body. The upavayus naga, koorma, krikara, devadetha and dhananjaya are helping to belching, blinking, sneezing and yawning etc. The four steps of pranayama are used for activating four chakras with the help of three bandhas. Keeping mind on heart chakra, breathe in filling the lungs fully feeling that heart chakra is activated by Prana. When holding in, Jalandhara bandha is taken feeling that throat chakra is activated by Udana. By breathing out bend the body forward to press the abdomen and hold Moola bandha feeling that base chakra is activated by Apana. Then hold out taking Uddiyana bandha feeling that navel chakra is activated by Samana.

In yoga different pranayamas are used for different purposes. (1) Soorya bhedi/ Chandra bhedi :- Soorya bhedi is breathing only through right nostril for heating the body and Chandra bhedi is breathing through left nostril for cooling the body. (2) Ujjayi for cleaning the throat by making a sound by rubbing the throat when breathe out. (3) Seelkari is breathing out through the gap of the teeth with a hissing sound. (4)Seethali is to cool the brain by breathing in through a pipe of the tongue. (5) Bhramari is used to tune the brain. This is done by breathing out with a humming sound blocking the ears with fingers to avoid outside sounds and keeping a small gap between the teeth to avoid vibration of it. (6) Bhasthrika for consumption of more oxygen. In this we start with 4+4 and then take speed as 3+3...2+2...1+1...1/2+1/2...1/4+14... and then changing to 4+4 3+3 and so on. (7) Moorcha is keeping air inside as far as possible after breathing in. (8) Plavini is used for floating on water by breathing out only a part of the air as 4.2.
