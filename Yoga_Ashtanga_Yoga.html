Ashtanga Yoga

What is Ashtanga Yoga ?

We are not able to trace the origin of yoga in India. In Vedas there is reference of yoga. About five thousand years before a sage named Pathanjali divided then yoga into eight parts. Thus the name ashtanga came into existence. Ashta means eight and anga means part. The first part is Yama having five things which were designed for the smooth running of the society.

(1)Non violence:- In word deed or thought we should not be violent towards any living being. Living being is defined as any one that starts its life, lives a life of any duration and finally perishes. Killing is violence. But mental torture is more violent than killing, as its effect is longer than the other. When we become angry or violent the negative hormones produced in our body will aggravate the conditions of any disease in us. Thus the angry man and the object of anger are both troubled by this action.

(2)Truth:- This is defined as anything giving benefit to a living being.

(3)Non- stealing:- Not taking possession of a thing without the permission of its owner.

(4)Controlled use of sex:- It is only for the preparation of next generation and not for lust.

(5)Not desiring to take possession of all things that come across:- When we desire to get a thing and not getting it, we will be disappointed. When we are not expecting anything, that which we get will be a great thing and this will lead to a contented life.

Niyama the second part also have five things.

(1)Cleanliness:- We must keep our body, dress and surroundings clean.

(2)Pleasant expression:- We must be always please looking and never be gloomy.

(3)Penance:- We must be ready to undertake any hard work for attaining the goal.

(4)Learning the past:- Learning the past history is essential for understanding the achievements of our predecessors. This will help us to proceed our work easily.

(5)Bestowing everything up on God:- We must understand God as the energy working in us and feel that all that we do is done by God. This feeling will help us to avoid worries connected with our failures because the failure or success is not ours. Therefore we are not excited by the success also.

After Yama and Niyama the third part is Asanas

This includes Postures, Bendhas, Kriyas and Mudras.

There are three important Bendhas in Ashtanga yoga.

1, Jalandhara Bendha:- (Picture on Page 22) The chin is touching on the chest for closing the glottis. This is for holding the breath in side.

2, Moola Bendha:- In this, sphincter muscle of anus is closed tightly. This is done while breathing out and holding out the air.

3, Uddian Bendha:- (Picture on Page 27) After breathing out making a vacuum inside the whole abdominal muscles are held back.

There are six important Kriyas in Ashtanga.

1, Nethi:- Pull in water slowly through the nose and allow it to go out through the mouth.

2, Dhauthi: - Devour soft cotton cloth, 4 inches width and 15 feet long soaked in boiled water from one end and pull it out to take out phlegm from stomach and esophagus.

3, Vasthi:- It is an artificial enima taken by yogies by inserting smooth bamboo pipes in the anus and immerse their body in water.

4, Thratakam:- Look at a point for a long time without blinking until tear comes out through the eyes.

5, Nauli:- Holding back the abdomen muscles as in uddian bandha press the palms on the thighs. Then two stomach muscles will be coming forward. When alternately pressing they will be moving round and round.

6, Kapalabhathi:- Breathe out forcefully through nose and hold back the front muscles of abdomen. Breathing in release it.

There are seven important Mudras in Ashtanga yoga.

1, Brahma Mudra:- (Page 24) Breathing out through mouth turn the face to the shoulder and open the mouth maximum so that the nerves at the neck are stimulated. Do it on the other side also.

2, Mahamudra:- (Page 29) Breathing out touch the forehead on the knee.

3, Yogamudra:- From lotus posture bend forward to touch the forehead on the ground.

4, Aswineemudra:- The sphincter muscle of Anus contracting and releasing several times.

5, Chinmudra:- Touch the tip of pointing finger on the middle line on the thumb and other three fingers straight.

6, Dhyanamudra:- Tips of pointing finger and thumb touch together

7, Khecharimudra: - Touch the back of the tongue on the palate
