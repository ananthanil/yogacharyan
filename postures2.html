Postures (Part- 2)

Part- 2

This part includes some advanced yoga postures for those who are not satisfied with the light exercises of part one. But for treating some diseases certain items of this part are also essential.

Some of the items like head standing must be practiced daily.

Bedha Paschimaothanasan

(Tied lotus) After taking lotus bring the hands through the back and hold on the big toes with fingers.

Yogamudra

(Yoga seal) Hold the lotus and keeping palms on knees bend the body to touc.h the forehead on the ground breathing out.

UdhithaPaschimaothanasan

(Lifted lotus) Lotus can be lifted up on palms or fingers.

Udhitadendasan

(Lifted straight legs):- Keep the legs straight. Place the palms or fingers on the ground and lift the body and legs.

Paschimaothanasan

(Back stretch ) After taking mahamudra on both sides, keep both the legs together feet closer. Hold on both the big toes with forefingers and thumbs and try to fold the elbows to touch the ground and forehead touching on the knees.

Ardhamalsyendrasan

(Spine twisting) Fold one leg so that the heel come under the opposite buttock. Fold the other leg and place the foot on the other side of the first knee. Push the second knee to the other side and take the hand through the opposite side to hold on the foot. Round the other hand on the back and turn the face to the back. Do it on the other side.

Gomukhasan

(Cow face) Sit folding both the knees so that each foot on the opposite side and one knee above the other. Take the hands to the back, one through bottom and the other through above and lock the fingers. Repeat on the other side changing the legs and hands.

Koormasan

(Tortoise posture) Keep the soles together, take both the hands to the back of the body taking them through the bottom of the thighs and try to touch the chin on the feet.

Vajrasan

(Diamond posture) From cat pose sit back touching the buttocks on the ground in between the feet, keeping knees together. Place the palms in the knees and sit back bone straight.

Supthavajrasan

(Sleeping diamond) Lying back from diamond pose keeping palms on the thighs.

Mayurasan

(Peacock pose) Standing on knees bend down to place the palms on the ground, fingers to the back and thumps to the sides. Move forward bringing the elbows to the abdomen. Try to balance the whole body on the elbows by lifting the legs one by one and keeping the body and legs in a line parallel to the ground.

Upadhanasanam

(Pillow posture) From leg to shoulder (Padakandharam) lie on back.

Vrischikasan

(Scorpion posture) From head standing posture, bring the palms to the sides as wide as the elbows. Lift up the head from the ground and bend the knees to bring the feet above the head.

Nauli

(Projection) Holding Uddyan bendha press on the knees with both the palms so that the two front muscles of the abdomen are projected.

Padakandharasan

(Leg on shoulder) Sit with straight legs. Bring one leg through the outside of the hand to the back of the head and bring the hands to the chest keeping palms together.

Garudasan

(Kite pose) Take one leg rounding the other so that the foot comes to the same side. Twist the forearms together so that the palms come together in front of the face. Repeat with the other leg and twist the forearms in the opposite way.

Anjaneyasanam

Stretch one leg forward and the other leg backward, touching buttocks on the ground. Keep palms together folding the elbows as if saluting.

Kukkutasanam

Cross both the legs in padmasan. Insert both the hands through the folding of legs. Then stand on the palms locking legs on the elbows.

Hasthaseershasan

(Standing on head and palms) Stand on crow posture and bring the head to the ground so that the head and palms become a tripod. Then lift up the legs from the elbows and stand body straight up.

Veerya sthambhanasan

(Head to ankle) Standing feet apart, turn the body to one side and bending the body forward bring the forehead to the ankle by folding the knee of that side. Repeat on the other side.

Kukkutasan

(Cock Posture) Insert hands through the PADMASAN, and stand on hands.

Hasthaseersham

(Standing on hands) Place the palms on the ground and lift up the body straight up.

Head standing (Seershasan)

Interlock the fingers or overlap the palms, place the hands on the ground so that the elbows and the palms are in the vertices of an equilateral triangle. Lift up the knees and bring the feet nearer so that the body weight is coming to the elbows and head. Standing thus for a few days the neck will be acquiring more strength to hold the body weight. Then lift up one leg from the ground folding the knee. Now the body weight is shared by the leg on the ground also. Now try to balance on the tripod of head and elbows by lifting up the other leg also from the ground. After getting balance on this stage, bring the knees up. In this stage the beginners can have the support of a wall for not allowing the body to fall to the other side. Then lift up the legs fully.
