Postures (Part- 1)

(1) Bhramanakam

(Rotation) Place one leg before the other, keep the palms on the the knees. Breathing out bend the body sideward and come to one knee and then move to the other knee. Breathing in come up. Rotate three or four times in one direction and then in the opposite direction.

(1) Mareechasan

Fold one leg and bring the knee to the chest. Take the hand on that side to the back rounding the folded leg through the front and the other hand directly to the back to lock the fingers. After three breathing time, release that and do it with the other leg.

(1) Januseersha

(Head to knee) Place one sole parallel to the opposite thigh. Hold on the toes of the straight leg with both the hands. Breathing out come down to the knee to touch it with the forehead. Repeat three times and then with the other leg.

(1) Ardhapadmasan

(Semi lotus) Place one foot over the opposite thigh, heel touching the abdomen and knee touching the ground. Sit erect and after three breathing release it and do it with the other leg.

(1) Padmasan

(Full lotus) After taking semi lotus on both sides slowly try to cross both the legs. Never take too much strain.

(1) Akarnadhanurasan

(Charged bow posture) Keep both legs straight, hold on big toes with both the hands. Then folding one leg bring the toe near the ear on that side. Repeat with other leg.

(1) Makarasan

(Crocodile) Support the chin with the palms, elbows touching on the ground, keeping the feet closer and the top of the toes touching the ground. This is a relaxation posture. In supthamakara, we can place palms one above the other and cheek over it.

(1) Bhujangasan

(Cobra) Place the palms on the ground on both sides with elbows close to the body. Breathing in, lift up the body up to the navel, with the help of back bone; breathing out come down to touch the chin on the ground.

(1) Dhanurasan

(Bow posture) Folding both the legs hold on the ankles with both the hands and then try to unfold the knees. Thigh and chest will come up and only the abdomen will touch on the ground.

(1) Salabhasan

(Locust) Place the hands on both sides, palms downwards and breathing in lift up one leg, keeping the knee straight. Breathing out bring it down. Do it with the other leg and then with both the legs together. Fists under the thigh will help to lift up both the legs together.

(1) Naukasan

(Boat pose) Bring both the hands to the back, one holding the other Lift the head and the legs as high as possible and rock up and down to give a massage to the stomach.

(1) Marjarasan

(Cat pose) Breathing in lower the spine and look up. Breathing out lift up the spine and bring the face down in between the arms.

(1) Svanasan

(Dog stretch) Stretch one leg back parallel to the ground and the opposite hand to the front. After three or four breathings release them and do it with other side.

(1) Sasankasan

(Rabbit) From cat pose, keeping palms on the same position sit back on the heels and touch the head on the ground with normal breathing.

(1) Adhomukhasvanasan

(Down Ward Dog) From cat pose lift up the knees and breathing out move the head through the middle of the hands so as to see the knees. Then the heels touch on the ground. Breathing in bring the knees to the ground and come to the first position.

(1) Ushtrasan

(Camel) Standing on knees hold on the ankles with both hands, project the body forward and look back. Some people can bring their head down to touch the soles.

(1) Warming up by standing

Stand stretching hands to the sides and breathing out twist the body and look back. Breathing in come to the front and go to the other side breathing out.

(2) Warming up by standing

Breathing in lift up both hands. Breathing out bend the body forward swinging the hands through the sides of the legs and finally push the hands at the back. Breathing in come up.

(3) Warming up by standing

Stand feet apart and move the hands through the middle of the legs instead of sides as elephant does with his trunk.

(4) Warming up by standing

Stand feet at shoulder distance and stretch hands to the sides. Breathing out bringing hands to the front sit down. Breathing in come up.

(1) Padahasthasan

(Hands to feet) Breathing in lift up both the hands. Breathing out bend the body forward and try to touch the palms on the ground.

(1) Thrikonasan

(Triangular posture) Standing feet apart, (distance between the feet nearly equal to the length of one leg) breathing out touch one foot with the palm of the opposite hand; lift up the other hand and Iturning the face up look at the lifted palm. Breathing in come to the first position and do it on the other side.

(1) Ardhachandrasan

(Half moon posture) Standing feet a little apart for keeping balance, lift up the hands and breathing in bend the body backward and look back.

(1) Sethubendhanasan

(Bridge Posture) Holding on the ankles lift up the middle portion. By supporting the buttock with palms we can lift up better.

(1) Sakatasan

(Cart Posture) Sit stretching legs forward, supporting the body with hands. Breathing in lift up the body keeping heals, buttocks and torso in one line and look backward.

(1) Ulkatasan

(Chair Posture) Stretching hands forward, bring down the body, bending the knees as if sitting in a chair.

(1) Padangushtam

(Fingers to the toes) Standing on one leg stretch the other leg to the front or side, holding on the big toe with the hand on that side.

(1) Bakasan

(Crane Posture) Keep palms on ground at shoulder distance spreading fingers fully well. Bring both the legs one by one through the sides of the hands and lift up the body and stand on hands looking forward.

(1) Aswachanchalasan

(Jumping Horse) From downward dog posture breathing in take one foot to the front in between the palms. Breathing out again go to downward dog posture and again breathing in take the other leg forward.

(1) Kakasan

(Crow posture) Place the inner sides of the knees on the outer sides of the elbows and try to lift up the toes from the ground and balance on palms.

Cycling

Keeping hands near the body, palms downwards, fold one knee breathing out and bring it over the chest. Breathing in release it but do not place it down. Keep it a little above the ground. Repeat with the otherfleg. Thus alternately do the exercise and then relax.

Merudendasan

(Back strain) Breathing in lift up one leg forty five degrees. Breathing out place it down. Repeat with the other leg and then with both the legs together.

(1) Pavanamukthasan

(Air releasing pose:-) Folding right leg hold on the knee with both the hands and bring it close to the chest. Breathing out lift up the head to touch the forehead on the knee. Breathing in release it. Then do it with the left leg and then with both the legs together.

Vipareethakarani

(Inversion posture) Lift up the legs and buttocks and support the buttocks with both the palms. The body will be resting on the head and top of the upper back.

Sarvangasan

(Shoulder standing) From Vipareethakarani bring the palms down to the ribs and keep the body straight. Then torso, heels and buttocks will be in one line.

(1)Malsyasan

(Fish posture). Keep the palms under the buttocks, tilt the face back so that the top of the head touches the ground curving up the back bone with normal breathing. This a complement for sarvangasan. This is improvised posture. Originally it can be done by lying back from Lotus posture.

(2)Halasan

(Plough posture) From Sarvangasan, bring down the feet to the back of the head to touch the toes on the ground. By bending the knees they come to the ears, which is another pose called Karnapeeda(Ear pressing).

Udarakarshanam

(Stomach Squeezing) inter lock the fingers and place the palms on the head. Fold the legs together so that the knees touch together. Breathing out tilt the knees to one side and the face to the opposite side. Do it on both sides.

Chakrasan

(Wheel) Bring the palms on the ground at the sides of the head and lift up the body on four legs.

Savasan

(Dead body posture) Keep the feet about two feet apart, hands about forty five degrees with the body, palms upwards, eyes closed and mouth closed. Feet may be slanting to the sides and fingers having natural curves. Shake the body to release all the muscles and make the breathing slow. After attaining slow breathing, devitalize the body when breath out and revitalize when breath in. To finish the relaxation, stretch right hand and folding it bring it under the head and turn the body fully to the right side and sit up. Rub the palms to heat them and place them on the closed eyes. Then open the eyes slowly.
