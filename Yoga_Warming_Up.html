Warming up Exercises

Any of the body part, if we don't use for a long time, will become useless. Therefor, in warming up exrices we give some movements for almost all joints of our body. Each movement is connected with breathing i.e. breathing in one movement and breathing out next movement; for eg. squeezing the toes breathing in and releasing them breathing out. We repeat each item three or five times for saving time. When practicing we bring our mind also to the body part where we are giving the exercise. After repeating each item we relax at least 5 or 10 seconds and at this time we evaluate its effects on our body part and understand how it is giving more blood circulation, more flexibility and more assimilation of Oxygen.

Following chapter is giving the description with images of warming up exercises and postures.

The part one contains easy postures for beginners but we have included essential postures for bending body forward, backward, and twisting.

Part -1

This part is intended for beginners of any age level. Sit on a mat stretching both the legs and if needed give a support for the body by keeping both the hands on the mat. Bring your mind also to the body part where the exercise is given. Do the movements connected with breathing that is breathing in one movement and breathing out another. For saving time it is advisable to repeat each item three or four times and after each item relax for a few seconds. During this time evaluate the effect of it. The criteria for evaluation are better blood circulation more flexibility and oxygenation. When breathing is full, the movement also must be complete. E.g. bending body forward breathing out is moving the body when breathing is also in progress. Thus each item must be synchronized with breathing. <
